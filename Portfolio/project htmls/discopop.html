DiscoPop

Mobile Computing • Gameplay Design • UI design • UX design

introduction

DiscoPop is a hectic two-player multiplayer game for co-located collaboration. The game is based on player-proximity in orded to break the mobile bubble. The innovative edge-to-edge technology allows the players ammo to spawn across the devices in a beautiful and unique way.

background

problem statement

As the usage of mobile apps and screen time incease, social interaction seems to decrease. Although many apps are collaborative in nature, such as social medias and games, they still correlate with increased isolation. The challenge for this project was to find a solution to break the mobile bubble, by designing a mobile application for collaborative interaction.

goals
"

How can we design a collaborative mobile app which encourages users to break the mobile bubble?

ideation

brainstorming

We brainstormed on a game based on edge-to-edge technology, where two users place the top edge of their phones against each other to augment their devices. The gameplay should be a hectic competitive shooting game where the users try to overwhelm the opponents side by shooting ammo while protecting their own side. This will, based on the edge-to-edge technology, allow users to see their ammo spawn across their device onto their opponents device. Each users controls four lanes on the device, which shoots ammo based on different swipe gestures.

what

A classic hectic two-player competitive mobile game, but based on player proximity.

why

Break the mobile bubble through competition and fun along with a collaborative asynchronous activity. A combination of a traditional game and but with a unique and original gameplay style through using edge-to-edge technology.

who

Two players; Anyone who downloads mobile games and isn't shy to try new innovative concepts.

when & where

Anytime and anywhere the users can physically be in the same location and have access to a internet connected device and a stable surface.

prototype

style guide

The overall UI design patterns were based on the Android Material Design Guidelines. The desired aesthetics for the game was futuristic, tech, retro and space-themed. To achieve style this we looked for deep and metallic colors but with a touch of playfulness and pop to enhance the retro space theme. We searched for a thick, bold and eye-catching display font to use as the primary font to match the futuristic theme, contrasted by a sharp, edgy thin secondary font to match the tech and robotic look.

wireframes

The prototypes includes wireframes of the welcome screens, connect to a player, gameplay tutorial and the gameplay. Design ideas that were considered but not prototyped were high scores and power-ups, such as cool downs and charges. As the wireframes had to follow the infrastructure of the code, which in this specific case was coded in Flutter Firebase, a lot of the design decisions had to be be based on the backend.
